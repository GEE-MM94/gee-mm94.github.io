Medical Advisory Group Services

Litigation is often a costly exercise, requiring investment of time and money, for all parties involved. Personal injury litigation and medical malpractice litigation in particular has been topical in the recent years. In the quest to attain justice the court has to rely on information obtained from medical experts. MAGS is a wholly black owned medical consultancy firm. It is founded and managed by medical experts with extensive and established experience in the various medical specialities and the application of same to malpractice litigation.

Our Services

Writing of reports. This includes primary, supplementary and joint minute reports.

Court appearances. Our consultants are available to serve as expert witnesses during court appearances.

We offer analyses on the prevalence and incidence of adverse health outcomes within the population of interest for our clients. The data is then analyzed further to discern patterns and advise on nature of the adverse outcomes and the inherent risks predisposing the clients to the risk of malpractice litigation. We subsequently advise on the appropriate interventions aimed at mitigating and or preventing the risks.

MAGS, offers access to mediation as an alternative dispute resolution mechanism. Mediation is far less hostile and more cost effective than litigation. It fosters the opportunity for the parties to attain amicable resolution of their disputes. This is attained by offering the parties a "sheltered" environment within which each can be heard.

Advise on primary prevention and early detection of the causes of malpractice litigation.

Support to clients in up skilling staff, in order to equip them with the skills required to lead to improved health outcomes and mitigation of the incidence of malpractice litigation.

Implementation of complementary technologies aimed at improved health outcomes and security of medical data(electronic medical records, digitisation of health records, data storage and analysis amongst others).

The incidence of medical malpractice litigation has increased sharply in the recent years. The increase is in both the incidence of new cases and the size of the quantum for various cases. Adverse events in the four disciplines, Obstetrics, Neonatology, Neurosurgery and Orthopaedics, accounts for more than 80% of the cases of medical malpractice. Obstetrics and Paediatrics account for the most frequent cases and the highest amount of quantum payable.

Our experts keep pace with advances within the healthcare industry. This enables them to apply their knowledge based both on experience and medical literature to advise our clients appropriately.

The range of experts associated with MAGS include senior lecturers and departmental heads in academic posts. MAGS is founded and directed by medical experts. For this reason our opinions are objective and are of high ethical standards.

We are not lawyers and thus have no material interest in the outcomes of the judicial processes. Our primary role is to review the medical data and to advise our clients on what the actual outcomes are, what the merits of the case are and whether or not the case should be settled or defended. As practitioners who are often confronted by adverse outcomes in our daily activities, we add value to our clients by helping to distinguish between those outcomes that could have been prevented from those that may have been inevitable even under ideal circumstances.

While brain damage(Cerebral Palsy) during pregnancy and or infancy is the commonest reason for most cases of medical malpractice litigation, not all cases are due to negligence. Of equal importance is that not all cases arise during labour and or delivery and not all cases are preventable. One of our principal objectives is to advise our clients in the healthcare industry on formulation of strategies aimed at triaging of the cases based on sound criteria. This information is then used to decide on prioritization of cases and the rationale for allocation of time, financial and human resources. To this extent our experts can advise on proactive measures that can be implemented to mitigate these.

“BiG Data” Analytics

The use of technology and “BiG Data” analytics gives MAGS the edge within the field of medical consultancy. In association with our partners, we offer services from data mining, storage and analysis. By advising our clients on route cause analysis we empower them to make decisions about both the causes and prevention of adverse outcomes that lead to medical malpractice litigation.

Our Rates

Our service offerings are priced competitively despite the the high quality and ethical standards of advise and interventions available to our clients. Rates vary depending on the type of medical discipline briefed. Other factors that influence our tariffs are the time required to prepare reports and the number of experts required for each case. We offer negotiated rates for bulk services or for cases where opinions may be required for the purposes of enabling quicker settlements, thus saving on legal costs.
